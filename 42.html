General features of linear programming problems
1.
The feasible region is always a convex region.
2.
The maximum (or minimum) solution of the objective function occurs at the vertex (corner)
of the feasible region.
3.
If two corner points produce the same maximum (or minimum) value of the objective
function, then every point on the line segment joining these points will also give the same
maximum (or minimum) value.
